# MTD assumption drift at the point of £50k mandation

**Case repository:** [`mtd-assumption-drift-2026`](https://github.com/antnewman/mtd-assumption-drift-2026)
**Status:** In progress (April 2026).
**Supabase schema:** `mtd_2026`.
**Anchor:** Public Accounts Committee, *Progress with Making Tax Digital*, HC 1333 (80th report of Session 2022-23, 24 November 2023), Conclusion 4 and the associated recommendation.

## Question

At the point of £50k mandation (6 April 2026), ten months after HMRC's June 2025 restatement of the Making Tax Digital business case, do the key assumptions still hold when scored against current public external data?

## Assumptions under investigation

1. Average transitional cost per £50k-cohort business: £285 (2021 prices). *Primary.*
2. Total transitional cost for above-£30k population: £561m (2021 prices).
3. In-scope population at the £50k threshold from April 2026: 780,000.
4. Self Assessment tax gap: 18.5% (approximately £5bn).
5. Overall MTD Programme return on investment: 3.8:1 (BCR 1.3:1).

## External series used

ONS services CPI (D7NN, D7F5), ONS all-items CPI (D7BT, D7G7) for disclosure, DBT Business Population Estimates 2025, HMRC Measuring Tax Gaps 2025, OBR Economic and Fiscal Outlook (March 2026), Bank of England Bank Rate.

## Mandatory caveats

1. Services CPI (D7NN) is the correct reprice index, not all-items CPI.
2. Business Population Estimates 2025 is classified "official statistics in development", not "accredited".
3. Tax gap data runs approximately two years in arrears; the check is of consistency, not of drift.
